You’re part of Flock Ewe because you’ve worked hard. You’ve helped take BAAAHS from a leaping lamb of an idea to a big ol’ low hanging disco balled BAAAHD ass. You are the people who are creating the BAAAHS community, and we do mean COMMUNITY.

As you arrive, even before you put up your tent, please introduce yourself to other flock members. There will be more than 60 of us in our camp, so we need to make ourselves known to one another. It’s really simple: make friends and make more than friends, because, for this week and beyond we are each other’s chosen BAAAHS family. Be awesome to each other. So let’s make whoopee, show each other our favorite body part, make memories, jump in and help however we can, you aren’t sure, ask.

As the largest queer mutant vehicle ever brought to Burning Man, this is a FIRST! And we are all a part of it!!!

Camp Rules

BE AMAZINGLY AWESOME. This is a BE AWESOME economy. Give some of your awesomeness to every person you encounter; you’ll get awesomeness back in return. We promise.

When in doubt, the answer is "yes"!

Keep the pigs at bay. Unless it’s role-play, getting arrested isn’t hot. Never bring law enforcement back to camp. The best way to do this is to avoid them altogether and don’t get in trouble. Be discreet in camp and out on the Playa.

How to Speak to Law Enforcement and other great tips can be found here (http://www.lawyersforburners.com/home).

If for some reason you encounter law enforcement, here a way to diffuse the situation:

  • Officer, my name is [give your real name].
  • Please explain to me your probable cause for stopping me, and if it seems reasonable to me, I will give you my personal identification information.
  • I politely refuse to give you any further information.
  • I do not consent to any search of me or my belongings, tent, vehicle or camp.
  • Am I free to go?
  • Remember, this is legal information not legal advice, which is an interpretation of the applicable law to specific circumstances. We urge you to consult a lawyer for legal advice about a particular legal question or issue you may have.

No smoking on the BAAAHS or in common camp areas. This is both for safety and courtesy. We don’t want to have a big ass amazingly awesome singed metal hulk... we’d have to change our acronym. Please limit cigarette smoking in camp to your personal areas, and let your visitors know we’d like to keep shared camp areas smoke-free.

No smoking illegal substances in public areas. That means pot. Top of the list. Even if you have a medical marijuana license. In Nevada, it’s still illegal. The quickest way to get a member of one of the multiple law enforcement agencies visiting you is a whiff of telltale Mary Jane.

Law Enforcement Officers (LEOs) have a pretty loose definition of public areas: under a shade structure, inside your tent with the door open, behind your car. All public areas are the same as the open playa in their eyes. We don’t want to tell you what to do, but we do want to help you not get a citation. There are lots of things you can do that don’t involve smoking pot.  If you do smoke pot, be discreet and be smart. Oh, and remember that part about not smoking on the BAAAHS? Ditto.

Law Enforcement and Illicit Substances. So I’m not saying that you are going to do illicit substances while you are at Burning Man, but if you happen to be contemplating some such activity …

Here are some simple tips:

  • In-the-open is bad. Don’t do anything in wide-open areas. Particularly at night-time. You may not see anyone but night vision goggles can clearly see you doing what you shouldn’t be doing. Remember LEOs have a loose definition of public areas.
  • Private is good. Go into a porta pottie. Go into a protected spot. Mummify yourself in your sleeping bag
  • Never on the bus. Very, very, very bad idea. Do not do it on the bus when it’s moving, when it’s still, when it’s in camp. Pearl does not do drugs. They upset her constitution.
  • Dark is better. If you do something in your tent, please, please, don’t do a Bunraku puppet show in there for all the world to see. Turn out your lights. You may think you are discreet but the shadows are giving us a blow by blow (so to speak).
  • No whippets. DUH! Cops know what these are and look for them lying around camps and then go and search your shit. Keep it in your tent, Mary.
  • Gifting/Selling. Um? Okay, it’s always nice to give gifts. But LEOs have this very backwards view of it called trafficking, distribution and sale. If it isn’t a good friend, don’t do it.

At the end of the day, remember the mantra, be discreet and be smart. I have seen too many people make a little slip and have it ruin the rest of their Burn. Or other people’s burn. Not very nice. ‘Nuff said.

Water. We will be providing water. The very essence of life itself. We will be planning for about 2 gallons of water per person per day. HOWEVER, we still recommend that you bring some water. Things happen. Water gets contaminated. We should be fine BUT if you have some in reserve that will help you or other folks. With that in mind, please bring 1 gallon of water per person per day. To best make use of the camp water supply, please consider bringing larger refillable water containers for your group. Fewer trips to the main tank will help with sanity and convenience. 5 gallon refillable water cubes are a great idea.

Gray water. We are still working on the specifics of gray water removal. We hope to have information to share at our All Flock Meeting.

Assorted other niceties. Bike Racks, Shade structures, maybe a Yurt.

It’s likely some groups will create their own kitchen space so it’s possible if you are very, very nice, they may share with you. Be respectful of their space and don’t presume. If you are naughty, well, consequences will ensue.

For ideas on what to bring and preparing meals on the playa – One of our fellow Flock members is working on some tips that we’ll be sharing shortly.

First Timers – Virgins

We love you. We wish we could be you again. Keep in mind Burning Man can be a wild ride. It’s misery and ecstasy. Have no expectations. The environment can be harsh (be very, very kind to your feet!). You can become easily over-stimulated (no comment). Just take it in and let it take you in. Don’t compare and contrast. At its core Burning Man will be your personal experience as part of the larger community. Take care of yourself.

Be Kind. Be kind to your friends and your loved ones. If you are bringing first timers, please watch out for them. And above all, drink water. If you are thirsty, you’re probably dehydrated. Piss Clear.

No MOOP. Old timers know this. Everything that comes to Burning Man must leave too. That means 100% of garbage. MOOP: Matter Out Of Place. We ask that throughout the week you make an effort to keep our camp clean. And when you pack up, you make sure your space is spotless. And I do mean spotless. Nothing on the ground that didn’t come there naturally. Not a nail (finger or otherwise), a sequin, a thread. Nothing. For more on what constitutes MOOP, please check this out.
