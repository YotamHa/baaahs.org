Roles

Event Producer

The event producer is responsible for logistics and ___ related to the event:

  • Make sure shifts are staffed ahead of setup.
  • Book any rental vehicles or heavy equipment needed.
  • Support setup lead during setup and teardown (not necessarily on site).

Setup Lead

The setup lead is ultimately responsible for making setup happen. They:

  • Ensure that all necessary hardware, tools, parts, and equipment are obtained and on site.
  • Are on site during setup and teardown.
  • Make sure all crews are properly staffed.
  • Set up work area (crew tables, parts and panel staging areas, craft services, worklights, music).
  • Manage arrival, placement, and departure of vehicles.
  • Manage any outside parties (vendors, clients, cops, concerned busybodies, etc).
  • Mind schedule, re-balance teams, call in reinforcements, etc.
  • Frequently check in with crew leads.
  • Watch for potential problems, mistakes, safety issues, etc.
  • Help debug any problems that arise.

Setting up for setup:

Crew tables: each of the crews (Party, Business, and Head & Hind) need a folding table near their workspace.

Tape the following sheets to each table:

  • Sign-in sheet
  • Shift signup list
  • Assembly instructions (that crew's section)
  • Bolt sizing chart
  • Panel map (that crew's)

Place these items on or under each table:

  • Gloves
  • Panel hanging totes (1/4" hardware, vice grips, aligner pins
  • Hand tools (socket wrenches, pliers, …)
  • Other hardware
  • Hardhats
  • Head lamps (if night)

Craft services: space for food, snacks, and drinks.

Safety: ensure that a well-stocked first aid kit and a fire extinguisher is on site their locations are well-known.

Crew Lead

There is a crew lead for each of the crews (Party, Business, and Head & Hind). Each crew lead is responsible for instructing (often inexperienced) crew members in safely assembling their part of the sheep. They should:

  • Read and understand the setup instructions ahead of time.
  • Be aware of the setup schedule.
  • Give crew members an overview of what they'll be working on, and communicate goals for the shift.
  • Demonstrate procedures (e.g. panel hanging).
  • Educate crew members on setup basics — sizes of hardware, names of tools, etc.
  • If issues arise, help debug them and pull in the setup lead.
  • Watch for any safety issues.

Fluffer

Each crew should have a fluffer. The fluffer picks up stuff their crew drops, grabs stuff on request, makes sure they're hydrated and taking enough breaks, etc.

Noms

Noms manages the craft services table. Keep cold drinks (water, soft drinks) and light snacks available throughout setup. Coffee and pastries in the AM. For shifts that overlap normal meal times, and for shifts of four hours or more, provide something more substantial like pizza or a sandwich platter.

Party Crew Setup(Driver) Side Setup

The Party Crew works mostly on the "party side", a.k.a. the driver side, of the sheep.

Overview:

  1. Install speaker shelves.
  2. Install DJ platform.
  3. Install mounting struts.
  4. Install initial mount panels.
  5. Install remainder of panels.

Install speaker shelves. For stationary setup, only one bolt (2"×½") is needed in each slot. First, slide center shelf into holes on side of bus. You’ll need to wiggle it to get it in. It should end up flush with the side of the bus. Next, install the right shelf and bolt to center shelf. Last, install the left shelf. For stationary setup, the shelves are only secure enough for the speakers OR for a single person at a time.

Requires:

Mobile: 9﹫2"×⅜" bolts.
Stationary: 4﹫2"×⅜" bolts.
Tools: 9/16" socket wrench, pliers, and aligner pin.

Install DJ platform.

The platform is in two sections, fore and aft. One edge of each is marked as “BUS SIDE”.

  1. The fore platform is installed first. Slide it into place under the aluminum steps, line up the holes, and bolt in place (finger-tight) to the dropped beam that’s inset two feet from the edge of the bus (2 bolts).
  2. Slide the aft platform in place. To align it properly, pull it away from the bus ½”, drop in place, and slide back toward the bus. Align the holes and bolt to the dropped beam (2 bolts).
  3. For mobile, add 5 additional bolts along the edge of the bus.
  4. Tighten all the bolts with wrenches.

Install panel support struts

Install panel support struts 7ABC, 17ABC, 17DEF, 33ABC, 4_ABC, and 41ABC. Tighten hardware with tools. Short ⅜" bolts at the ends of the struts should point up.

Install the panels.

Labeling: Each party (driver) side panel has a round red tag with its panel number. Each panel also has a tape label with its number, the numbers of its neighbors, and an arrow indicating which way is up. If tags or labels are illegible or missing, let Xian know!

Install panels 7, 17, and 21.

  1. Panel 7 sits on strut 7ABC. Use a short ⅜" bolt, finger-tight.
  2. Panel 17 sits on struts 17ABC and 17DEF. Use short ⅜" bolts, finger-tight.
  3. Panel 21 connects to a punched angle iron bracket on the DJ platform. Use a short ⅜" bolt, finger-tight. The other end attaches to panel 17. Use ¼" hardware, finger-tight.

Requires:

• Lots of 1/4" bolts, various lengths, with wing nuts.
• Lots of heavy-duty zip ties.
Tools: vice grips, aligner pins.

Illustration of panel labels

The rest of the panels go up and come down in groups as indicated below. For stationary setup, use heavy-duty zip ties wherever possible. In some cases zip ties may not be strong enough and ¼" hardware (bolt, two washers, and a wingnut) will be necessary.

  1. Raise the panel or group of panels into place.
  2. Align edges of panel and use tapered steel pin to line up holes. Pick a hole as near to the end as possible while still allowing hardware to be inserted. Try to reuse holes from previous installations if possible.
  3. Use a vice grip to clamp the panel to its neighbor.
  4. Remove the steel pin and replace with a heavy-duty zip tie or ¼" hardware.
  5. Remove vice grip.
  6. Repeat for the other end of the edge.
  7. Repeat for other vertices on the panel or panel group.

Two teams may install the front and rear panels simultaneously.

  • Fore Hoof (10 — inset a bit behind the other panels, attach with zip ties)

Install remaining rear panels in this order:

  • Ribs (27/28/29)

  • Leg (33/36/34/37/43 — panel 33 sits on strut 33ABC. Use a short ⅜" bolt, finger-tight.)
  • Hock (35/41/42 — be ready to attach strut 41)

Safety Checks:

  • All bolts are tight.

Head Assembly

Install face mounting bracket. Mounts on Arch #1 from top platform. Hardware: four 3"×¼" bolts through top, two 3"×¼" bolts through bottom.

Install face. Need: 10’ rope, L and R face support struts; small aligner pin; two 2"×¼" bolts; four wood screws and drill.

  1. Set face on the top platform facing forward, and tilted back so he’s looking upward. Attach L and R face support struts to the 5-hole punched angle iron bracket on the brow (use the outer holes, attach with ¼" hardware). Tie rope to brow between struts.
  2. With the face still tilted back so he’s looking forward and upward, slide the face along the platform, then along the face mounting bracket until the back edge of his chin rests at the edge of the mounting bracket. There are safety stops to prevent the face from sliding off the edge.
  3. Holding onto the L and R face support struts and the rope, tilt the face forward until he’s looking straight ahead. Secure the face (at the back of his chin) to the mounting bracket using two ¼" bolts.
  4. Secure the L and R face support struts to the platform where indicated using two wood screws for each strut. Tie the rope to any secure spot on the platform.

Install ears.

Need: L and R aluminum head support studs; L and R ears; F16D/F20D/F17D and F16P/F20P/F17P panels (preassembled); aligner pin; impact driver with 9/16" deep socket; pliers; nine 1.5"×⅜" bolts; 1’ length of rope; four 4"×⅜" bolts; two 10’ lengths of rope.

Repeat for D(L) and P(R) sides:

  1. On the top platform, attach the F16/F20/F17 panel assembly to the aluminum head support stud using three 1½"×⅜" bolts. Attachment points are marked on the stud and on the panels.
  2. Slide the punched angle iron protruding from the ear through the forward hole in the F16/F20/F17 panel assembly and bolt it to the stud.
  3. Attach the back of the ear to the panel (using a ¼" bolt on the P side and a short rope on the D side).
  4. Mount the aluminum head support stud (with panels and ear already attached) to Arch #1 using two 4"×⅜" bolts. The stud sits on the outside of the 2"×2" arch column, resting on the lower 1"×1½" part of the column.

Install head.
